✦ Overview

How the competition works

Core information for students and families to navigate ACSL with confidence.

👥 Team setup

  • 3-Score Team: final score uses the top 3 individual results, up to 6 members.
  • 5-Score Team: final score uses the top 5 individual results, up to 10 members.
  • Teams are placed according to division and student experience level.

📌 Competition structure

  • Regular season includes 4 online contests completed individually.
  • Short-answer tasks and, depending on division, a programming challenge in HackerRank.
  • ACSL is an institutional member of CSTA and supported by Google and HackerRank.

⚠️ Important: the competition is entirely in English with no official translation during tests.

✦ Divisions

ACSL participation tracks

Each division has specific formats and challenge levels tailored to students' academic stage.

✏️ Elementary Division

For grades 3 to 6. A 30-minute online test with 6 theoretical questions (no programming).

  • Computer number systems
  • Prefix, infix and postfix notation
  • Boolean algebra and graph theory

Sample test

💻 Junior Division

For grades 7 to 9. Includes a 30-minute theory test plus a programming problem (up to 72 hours).

  • Recursion, LISP and data structures
  • Finite automata and regular expressions
  • Digital electronics and assembly language

Short problems | Programming

📈 Intermediate Division

For high school students with limited coding experience or advanced middle school students.

  • 6-question theory test
  • Programming challenge with extended submission window
  • Topics aligned with the intermediate ACSL pathway

Division sample

🥇 Senior Division

For high school students with strong programming background, especially AP Computer Science profiles.

  • Same topic families as Intermediate, with higher difficulty
  • Theory + programming workflow in HackerRank
  • Strong emphasis on precision and speed

Division sample

✦ Academic Integrity

Conduct and honesty rules

ACSL requires fair individual performance throughout every stage.

✅ Allowed and not allowed

  • Paper, pencil and pen are allowed for notes.
  • Calculators are not allowed during tests.
  • Internet searches and external help are not allowed.

⚠️ Plagiarism and collaboration

  • HackerRank requires an individual-authorship agreement before the test.
  • First plagiarism flag: zero score on the affected question.
  • Repeated violation: disqualification from finals participation.
  • Team collaboration is encouraged only after the official test window closes.
